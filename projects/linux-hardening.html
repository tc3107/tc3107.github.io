Case Study

Security Operations & Hardening

Practical security work in support of live operations: reducing exposure, protecting access, monitoring for problems, and keeping business systems recoverable and usable.

Context

Security work in a small business environment is part of normal operations rather than a separate isolated stream. Access, updates, logs, backups, firewalling, and user support all affect real uptime and real business risk.

Problem / Goal

The goal is to maintain a secure and reliable baseline without introducing fragile controls or unnecessary friction for employees. Security has to support operations, not fight them.

My Role

I handle practical security-minded administration across the environment: access control, firewalling, hardening, monitoring, alerting, backup awareness, logs, and incident-aware troubleshooting as part of my broader systems role.

What I Built or Maintained

  • Account and permission controls for systems and services.
  • Firewalling and exposure review around business-facing and administrative services.
  • Monitoring and alerts tied to service health, storage issues, and availability risks.
  • Operational routines around updates, logs, service checks, and recovery readiness.
  • Security-conscious documentation that explains workflows without disclosing sensitive implementation details.

Tools / Technologies

Linux, SSH, firewalling, service logs, monitoring and alerting workflows, backup systems, access control practices, and administrative documentation.

Security / Reliability Considerations

This portfolio intentionally omits exact architecture, internal addressing, credentials, full firewall policies, and sensitive configuration. The underlying work is guided by least privilege, recoverability, patch awareness, operational monitoring, and the assumption that backups and logs matter as much as perimeter controls.

What This Demonstrates to Employers

I approach security as part of dependable operations. That translates well to infrastructure, support, platform, and security-adjacent roles because it requires careful troubleshooting, risk awareness, communication, and disciplined maintenance habits.

Skills Demonstrated

Access control Firewalling Hardening Monitoring Logs Backups Incident-aware troubleshooting Documentation Security-minded administration
